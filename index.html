Sitakunda Travel:

Hill, Waterfall, Beach - All In One Place!

Sitakunda (সীতাকুন্ড) is an upazila in Chittagong which is currently at the peak of popularity among tourists. Fountains, mountains, Waterfalls, Beach, clouds, you can enjoy everything from here? Actually, this is the reason why innumerable tourists are going to Sitakundo every day. Some are camping while others are trekking and coming back day after day. Sitakunda has gained more popularity as it is possible to return from Dhaka, Chittagong, Feni, Comilla or any other district in Bangladesh by day trip.harjhari Jharna Trail, komoldoho Jharna Trail and Sitakunda Ecopark. A traveler must stay for a few days to see all the sights of Sitakunda and its surroundings. If one starts the trail from Feni, it will take him 3 days to see the Boro komoldoho waterfalls, Choto komoldoho waterfalls, napittachara, khayachara, sahasradhara and suptadhara lasyamayi springs. Sitakunda has Banshbaria and Guliyakhali beaches. In addition to all this, if you want to trek separately in Hazarikhil or Baraiadhala forest, it will take more time, right? The hill of holy Chandranath is also not to be missed. From the capital Dhaka, Comilla, Narsingdi, Feni, Chittagong or its environs, you can easily reach to Sitakunda in one day. Some Places of Sitakunda:

Sitakunda is an ideal place to see the mountains, sea and lake in a day! There is a fountain as a bonus!

1. Chandranath Hill

Noise-free solitude far from the city, greenery all around, occasional call of animals and cool air. This thrilling feeling will give you a mountain of peace of mind. Yes, I was talking about Chandranath hill in Sitakunda. If you like to walk on inaccessible mountain paths, Chandranath hill is for you. Chandranath Hill is 4 km from Sitakunda Bazar in Chittagong. A hill to the east is one of the most popular trekking routes for visitors. The height of Chandranath hill is approximately 1020 feet. There are two ways to climb Chandranath hill. The road on the right is almost entirely of stairs and the road on the left is entirely a mountain path, with some broken stairs. It’s easy to get up on the left side and down the stairs on the right, but you can use the path as you wish.

2.Chandranath Temple

After about 1 hour – 1.5 hours trek you will see Sri Sri Virupaksha Temple. Every year a special puja is performed in this temple on the day of Shivratri i.e. Shivartudashi. A huge fair is held at Sitakunda centered on this puja. Hindus living in the Chandranath Hills area of ​​Sitakunda organize a big fair every year in the month of Bengali Falgun (English February-March). Which is known as Shivartudarshi Mela. Numerous saints and men and women from different parts of the world including Bangladesh, India, Nepal, Bhutan and Thailand participated in this fair. About 150 feet away from the Birupaksha temple is the Chandranath Temple which is located on the top of Chandranath hill. About 100 feet of this 150-foot road you will have to climb a steep hill where it is very difficult to control yourself. Standing on the top of Chandranath hill, you will see the sea on one side and the solitude of the hill on the other side. You will be fascinated and look at the green trees of the high and low hills. Eyes will be covered in peace.

3.Napittachora waterfall

Mirsarai’s Napittachara spring in Chittagong is easily a very beautiful spring. The interesting thing is that there are three waterfalls in a very short distance. The three springs are Kupikatakum, Mithachari and Bandarkum. And the path to the waterfalls is probably called Napittachara. If you want, you can take someone from there as a guide.

How To Go To Sitakunda?

You may depart for Sitakunda by night train or bus at night to get a full day for sightseeing.You can reach Sitakunda directly by bus from Dhaka to Chittagong. AC / Non-AC buses of various companies including S. Alam, Shyamoli, Saudia, Unique, Hanif, Eagle and ENA run from Sayedabad, Fakirapul, Mohakhali bus stand go in a regular basis. Tickets for non-AC buses cost from Tk 420 to Tk 480 per person and for AC buses from Tk 800 to Tk 1,100. If you want to go to Sitakunda by train from Dhaka, you have to get off at Feni railway station and from there you have to go to Feni Mahipal bus stand by rickshaw / auto for 10-15 Taka. You can take a local bus from Mahipal bus stand to Sitakunda for 50-60 Taka. However, at 10:30 pm you can go directly to Sitakunda from Kamalapur railway station by mail train fare of 120 Taka per person. In this case, if you want to get a seat, you have to sit on the train long before. Or if you want to come from another district, come to Sitakunda in such a way that you can come to Sitakunda in the morning. This will have time to walk around all day.The city of Sitakunda will fall before entering Chittagong when leaving Dhaka. You can go to Sitakunda by any bus from Dhaka to Chittagong. Then you can go to Sitakunda by CNG or autorickshaw from Fakirhat bus stand.
